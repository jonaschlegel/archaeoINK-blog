## Abstract

Sylvie Lewuillon explores the rise of archaeological illustrations in the 19th century and their significance in the development of modern scientific practices. This article, published in _Antiquity_, highlights how visual representations became a key tool in archaeological research, enabling more detailed documentation and analysis of artifacts. The article provides historical context, tracing how illustration techniques evolved alongside scientific advances in archaeology.

## Purpose and Audience

This article is aimed at historians of science, archaeologists, and scholars interested in the development of visual methodologies in archaeology. It offers insights into the role of illustration in the scientific practices of the 19th century and how these illustrations helped shape modern archaeological research.

## Key Excerpt

"Archaeological illustrations in the 19th century were more than artistic representations; they were scientific tools that allowed researchers to capture and communicate complex data about ancient artifacts and sites, thereby contributing to the professionalization of archaeology as a discipline."
