## Abstract

This guide, authored by Hazel Martingell and Alan Saville, provides a comprehensive overview of the techniques used to illustrate lithic artifacts, specifically stone tools, for specialist archaeological reports. Published as part of the Association of Archaeological Illustrators and Surveyors' report series, this resource outlines the best practices for accurately drawing stone tools, with an emphasis on technical precision and consistency for academic and research purposes.

## Purpose and Audience

This report is designed for archaeologists, illustrators, and researchers involved in the documentation and study of lithic artifacts. It offers detailed instructions on how to depict stone tools accurately, making it an essential resource for those preparing illustrations for specialist reports in archaeology.

## Key Excerpt

"Accurately illustrating lithic artifacts requires both technical skill and a deep understanding of the tool's context within archaeological research. The precision in drawing ensures that the artifact’s characteristics are preserved and communicated clearly for analysis and interpretation."
