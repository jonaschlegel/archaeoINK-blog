## Abstract

"Drawing Archaeological Finds: A Handbook" is a practical guide to the techniques and methods used in the illustration of archaeological finds. Written by Nicola Griffiths, Antony Jenner, and Christine Wilson, this handbook provides comprehensive instructions on how to accurately represent artifacts for publication and research. It covers a range of drawing methods suitable for various types of artifacts and emphasizes the importance of precision and clarity in archaeological illustration.

## Purpose and Audience

This book is designed for archaeologists, illustrators, and students who need guidance on how to depict archaeological finds accurately. It serves as both a practical manual and a reference for those involved in the documentation and publication of artifacts in the field of archaeology.

## Table of Contents

4. Preparing Illustrations for Publication
5. Case Studies: Drawing Pottery, Metalwork, and Stone Tools

## Key Excerpt

"An accurate and detailed drawing of an artifact is not only a means of documentation but also a vital tool for interpretation and research. The ability to represent finds clearly and correctly can significantly enhance their scholarly value."
