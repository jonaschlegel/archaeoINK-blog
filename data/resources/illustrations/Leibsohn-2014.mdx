## Abstract

In this article, Dana Leibsohn reviews _Past Presented: Archaeological Illustration and the Ancient Americas_, edited by Joanne Pillsbury. The review focuses on the role of archaeological illustration in shaping our understanding of ancient American cultures. Leibsohn evaluates the book's exploration of the historical and visual techniques used in archaeological illustrations, emphasizing its contributions to both scholarship and visual culture.

## Purpose and Audience

This article is aimed at archaeologists, art historians, and scholars interested in the visual representation of ancient cultures, particularly those of the Americas. It provides an insightful critique of the book's treatment of archaeological illustrations, making it a valuable resource for those studying visual methodologies in archaeology.

## Key Excerpt

"Archaeological illustrations are not just passive reproductions of objects; they actively shape our knowledge of the past. In the case of the Ancient Americas, these visual reconstructions allow us to engage with histories that would otherwise remain obscured."
